The thesis

A dashboard shows you margin fell. A digital twin tells you why — and what to do about it.

Platform (advertising plus cloud) is the mature, high-margin cash engine. Frontier (AR and AI devices) is the bet: heavy R&D, negative margin, justified by option value. Platform funds Frontier, and every quarter that arrangement costs more. Consolidated operating margin falls from 30.3% to 22.4% — but Platform operating income rises from $12.1B to $14.7B. The margin story is a capital-allocation choice, and this model lets you interrogate it.

What it does

Six systems, one canonical build path.

01 · FORECASTING

Ensemble projection with honest uncertainty

ARIMA, Prophet, and XGBoost run against every driver, weighted by walk-forward backtest error. The shaded bands are ~80% and ~95% confidence intervals, sized from residuals and widened where the models disagree. Wide bands are information, not decoration.

02 · SCENARIO LAB

What-ifs that propagate through the whole model

Every scenario is a single assumption override. A marketing cut lowers spend, so logo adds fall, so next year's cloud revenue drops — margin up now, growth down later. That second-order effect is the whole reason driver-based planning beats a static spreadsheet.

03 · THE ENGINE ROOM

Statements that balance by construction

A fully linked P&L, balance sheet, and cash flow across two segments and a consolidated view. Net income rolls to retained earnings; indirect cash flow reconciles to the balance-sheet cash line. No plug — the sheet ties out to $0.00003 across 36 months. A star-schema warehouse (SQLite plus a Parquet gold layer), a typed FastAPI service, a Streamlit dashboard, and an automated QBR / board / earnings writer all read from it.

Why it maps to the job

Each component is a strategic-finance competency you can point at.

Three-statement modeling
Fully linked, segment-level, balances by construction. The literal core skill of FP&A, demonstrated in code that ties out.
Capital allocation
Cash cow funding a growth bet. The two-segment design forces the exact question a strategic-finance team exists to answer.
Driver-based planning
Scenarios as assumption overrides. Second-order effects propagate, so the what-if is real rather than cosmetic.
Variance analysis
Price / volume / mix bridges. The decomposition that turns "revenue missed" into "here's exactly why."
Forecasting
Ensemble with backtested weights and confidence bands. Rigor about uncertainty, not a single false-precision number.
Executive communication
Auto-generated QBR, board memo, earnings. Narrated from live numbers — the last mile most models skip.
